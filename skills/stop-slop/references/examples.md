# Before/After Examples

## 1. Throat-Clearing + Binary Contrast

**Before:**
> Here's the thing: it's not about having the right tools. It's about having the right judgment.

**After:**
> Good judgment matters more than good tools.

## 2. Filler + Unnecessary Reassurance

**Before:**
> At its core, this is genuinely one of the most important challenges facing engineering teams today. Let that sink in.

**After:**
> Engineering teams struggle with this.

## 3. Business Jargon Stack

**Before:**
> We need to take a step back, unpack the landscape, and do a deep dive into what's really moving forward here.

**After:**
> We need to examine the situation.

## 4. Dramatic Fragmentation

**Before:**
> Speed. That's it. That's the whole strategy. One metric. One obsession. Everything else is noise.

**After:**
> The entire strategy optimizes for speed.

## 5. Rhetorical Setup

**Before:**
> What if the problem isn't technical debt at all? What if it's something deeper? Think about it: every team you've ever worked on has faced this exact moment.

**After:**
> Most teams blame technical debt when the problem is unclear ownership.
